# Boundaries

Boundary data gives the repository one explicit framing layer for countries,
regions, and scope-aware filtering. That sounds modest, but it matters because
mixed evidence maps become much harder to trust when readers cannot tell what
geography a filter actually means.

## What Boundaries Contribute

- country outlines for public maps
- stable scope framing for world, regional, and country bundles
- readable filtering rules for geography-driven views

## Where To Inspect It

- `data/boundaries/normalized/` is the governing repository path for these
  framing layers
- the main job of this family is country filtering and regional scope control,
  not historical proof

## What Boundaries Do Not Claim

Boundaries are not scientific evidence. They are a framing and interpretation
surface. Their job is to keep public geography legible, not to prove an ancient
DNA or archaeology claim on their own.

## Use This Page When

- you want to know why a map filter is trustworthy even though it is not
  evidence
- you need the path behind a framing layer
- you want the plain explanation of how country filtering differs from sample
  or archaeology evidence
