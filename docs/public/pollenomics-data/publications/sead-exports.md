# SEAD Exports

These outputs are the repository-owned environmental archaeology context
surfaces derived from SEAD. They help the public product explain archaeology
context without confusing it with sample-backed ancient DNA evidence.

SEAD matters because readers often need archaeology context that is broader
than one country and broader than one excavation-like reading. These exports
help the repository explain settlement and environmental activity around a
geography without pretending that archaeology context and direct sample evidence
are the same thing.

## What Questions These Exports Help Answer

- what wider archaeology context surrounds a visible geography
- whether a public interpretation is grounded only in pollen and ancient DNA or
  also in broader archaeology context
- how a reader should think about archaeology patterns outside the narrowest
  country frame
- why a public region can be interesting even when direct sample density is not
  yet strong everywhere

## What SEAD Adds To The Public Product

These exports make the public product more useful by adding:

- broader environmental archaeology framing
- cross-regional context that keeps interpretation from collapsing into one
  evidence family
- a way to compare archaeological background across visible geographies

## Where To Inspect It

- `data/sead/normalized/` is the governing repository path for these SEAD
  contextual outputs
- this page explains how that normalized archaeology context becomes visible in
  reports and maps without pretending to be direct sample evidence

That helps readers ask better questions. Instead of "is there one sample here,"
they can also ask "what kind of surrounding archaeology context does this place
sit inside."

## What They Do Not Prove

SEAD exports are contextual publications. They should not be read as if they:

- prove one specific site-level or sample-level claim on their own
- replace chronology, locality, or coordinate review for sample-backed rows
- make a thin ancient DNA surface strong simply by surrounding it with richer
  archaeology context

## When A Reader Should Open This Page

Open this page when:

- a report or map seems archaeology-rich and you want to know what family is
  providing that context
- you want to understand the repository beyond the sample-only view
- you are thinking about adapting the publication model to another region and
  need to understand what a broad archaeology support family contributes

Then continue to [maps](maps.md), [reports](reports.md), or
[SEAD source guidance](../sources/sead.md) depending on whether the question is
about the visible product or the source family itself.
